1. Overview

This PR updates the GOLD.CUSTOMER_LIFETIME_VALUE mart to extend the CLV projection horizon from 3 years to 5 years and introduces a finer-grained churn risk segmentation. The change is confined to a single Gold-layer mart (28_customer_lifetime_value.sql) with a full-refresh strategy, requiring no upstream schema modifications in RAW or SILVER. Downstream consumers of CUSTOMER_LIFETIME_VALUE must account for the renamed column and revised churn band enumeration.

#### CTE: return_stats *(unchanged)*

Joins SILVER.RETURNS to SILVER.ORDERS to attribute return counts and refund amounts back to CUSTOMER_ID. No status filter is applied here; all returns are included regardless of originating order status.

#### Final SELECT — PROJECTED_CLV_5YR *(changed)*

The projected CLV calculation annualizes revenue over the customer lifespan and multiplies by the projection horizon:

-- Conceptual form (multiplier changed from 3 to 5)
(LIFETIME_REVENUE / NULLIF(CUSTOMER_LIFESPAN_DAYS, 0)) * 365 * 5

This is a linear extrapolation; no decay or discount rate is applied. The 5-year window now aligns with typical enterprise CLV reporting conventions.

#### Final SELECT — CHURN_RISK_BAND *(changed)*

Previous 3-band logic:

DAYS_SINCE_LAST_ORDER <= 30   → LOW
DAYS_SINCE_LAST_ORDER <= 90   → MEDIUM
else                           → HIGH

New 4-band logic:

DAYS_SINCE_LAST_ORDER <= 30   → LOW
DAYS_SINCE_LAST_ORDER <= 60   → MEDIUM
DAYS_SINCE_LAST_ORDER <= 120  → MEDIUM_HIGH   ← new
else                           → HIGH

The MEDIUM_HIGH band captures customers in an early re-engagement window (61–120 days inactive) who are at elevated but not terminal churn risk. The HIGH band now represents customers inactive for more than 120 days, making it a stricter definition than before.
